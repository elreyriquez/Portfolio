Case Study

FX Sesh

Your foreign exchange market clock. Session awareness at a glance. A mobile app and companion website for traders.

Problem

  • Traders juggle four global sessions (Sydney, Tokyo, London, New York) and constant time-zone confusion.
  • Most tools are web-only, cluttered, or bury session info inside full trading platforms.
  • People waste mental energy on "when does London open?" instead of focusing on the trade.

Approach

  • Mobile-first experience that answers one question: when are the sessions, and what's open right now?
  • Improve clarity and readability.
  • Create a scalable and consistent design system.
  • Brand extended to a marketing site that drives installs and explains value.

Key design decisions

The interface was structured with a clear visual hierarchy, ensuring key information is immediately visible while secondary elements remain accessible without overwhelming the user.

Spacing, typography, and color were used intentionally to guide attention and reduce cognitive load.

UI Elements

  • Bottom tab navigation (Sessions, Journal, News, Learn, Settings)
  • Open/closed session status badges
  • Digital and analog clock displays
  • Countdown timer components
  • Per-session notification toggles
  • Ad banner strips (non-intrusive, free tier)
  • Modal sheets for journal entry
  • Home screen widget (Android)

Outcome

  • FX Sesh live on App Store and Google Play with a companion website for discovery and support.
  • Built with Expo/React Native (iOS + Android from one codebase) and a static marketing site for fast, reliable presence.
  • Freemium model: ad-supported free tier live, premium subscription with journal, widget, pre-alerts, and overlap notifications in beta.

Companion website (also designed by me)

I designed the FX Sesh marketing site to match the app: same brand, clear value prop, and download CTAs so visitors can go straight to the store or beta.

User journey map

This map reflects the real app flow: users open FX Sesh for session awareness first, then branch into reminders, journaling, news, learning, and re-entry via widget or notifications.

Decision-making wireframe

This flow shows how the product guides users based on intent: checking sessions, enabling reminders, logging trades, reading news, or moving into learning content without forcing a single path.

Key Experience Decisions

  • Session at a glance: Digital and analog clock views with swipe; open/closed state and countdown to next open/close.
  • Controlled notifications: Per-session toggles and optional pre-alerts so users stay informed without spam.
  • Progressive depth: Sessions first; then Journal, News, and Learn tabs for users who want more.
  • Consistent experience: Same brand across the app and the FX Sesh website.

Beta testing process

I ran a focused beta-testing phase by reaching out to 15 testers and asking them to use the app in real trading scenarios, then share opinions on clarity, ease of use, and overall usefulness. Their feedback directly shaped the next round of updates: I refined UI details, improved screen clarity, and fixed bugs and functionality issues that surfaced during real use. That process helped move FX Sesh from a concept that worked in theory to a product that felt more stable, more intuitive, and more aligned with how traders actually check sessions, manage reminders, and navigate the app day to day.
